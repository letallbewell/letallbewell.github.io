Differential Equations

Green's Function

Consider a linear differential operator \( \mathcal{L} \) appearing in the ordinary differential equation (ODE), $$ \mathcal{L} f(x) = g(x), $$ with homogeneous boundary conditions \( f(\pm L) = 0\) with the possibility of \( L \) being infinite. \(g\) is called the source term and \(f\) is our solution. The Green's function, \( G(x, x^{'}) \) is the solution to the ODE for a delta function source term, $$ \mathcal{L} G(x, x^{'}) = \delta(x, x^{'}) .$$ If we apply \( \int dx g(x) \) on both sides, $$ \int \mathcal{L} G(x, x^{'}) g(x) dx = \int \delta(x, x^{'}) g(x) dx.$$ We can pull \( \mathcal{L} \) out of the integral since it is linear and evaluate the delta function integral to give, $$ \mathcal{L} \int G(x, x^{'}) g(x) dx = g(x^{'}).$$ Evidently, we achieved the soultion in abstract form as $$ f(x^{'}) = \int G(x, x^{'}) g(x) dx .$$ To do: 1 ODE and 1 PDE example
